    Poaching is the illegal hunting, capturing, or killing of wildlife, often targeting protected species for their valuable parts like ivory, horns, or fur. It disrupts ecosystems, endangers species, and undermines conservation efforts, driven by motives such as commercial gain, traditional medicine, and the exotic pet trade.

    Habitat destruction means ruining or damaging the natural homes of plants and animals. This happens when people change or destroy places where these creatures live, like chopping down forests, building cities, or polluting rivers. It's a big problem because it can make animals and plants homeless and even lead to their extinction.

    Climate change and pollution significantly harm wildlife, disrupting habitats and food sources. Rising temperatures, altered precipitation, and pollution lead to habitat loss, disease spread, and biodiversity decline, endangering species' survival and ecosystem balance.
